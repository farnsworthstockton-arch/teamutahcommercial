Common Operating Expenses Explained

Property taxes are assessed by the county based on the property's appraised value. In Utah, commercial properties are taxed at 100% of fair market value. Rates vary by county and taxing district, but tenants in NNN leases pay their pro-rata share based on leased square footage.
Typical Utah Range: $2.50 - $5.00 /SF/Year
Building insurance covers the structure, liability, and sometimes loss of rents. Tenants typically carry their own contents and liability insurance. In NNN leases, the tenant's share of building insurance is passed through as an additional cost.
Typical Utah Range: $0.75 - $2.00 /SF/Year
CAM covers shared expenses like parking lot maintenance, landscaping, snow removal, exterior lighting, shared restrooms, and common area repairs. CAM charges are allocated pro-rata among tenants. Look for CAM caps in your lease to limit annual increases.
Typical Utah Range: $3.00 - $7.00 /SF/Year
Utilities include electricity, gas, water, and sewer. In some buildings, tenants have separate meters and pay directly. In others, utilities are shared and allocated by square footage or sub-metered. Utah's relatively low utility rates help keep these costs manageable.
Typical Utah Range: $1.50 - $4.00 /SF/Year
Janitorial services cover cleaning of common areas, restrooms, lobbies, and sometimes tenant spaces (especially in full-service leases). Office buildings typically include janitorial; industrial and retail tenants usually handle their own cleaning.
Typical Utah Range: $1.00 - $2.50 /SF/Year
Property management fees cover day-to-day operations, tenant coordination, vendor management, and financial reporting. Fees are typically 3-6% of gross rental income and may be included in CAM or billed separately depending on the lease structure.
Typical Utah Range: $0.50 - $2.00 /SF/Year
