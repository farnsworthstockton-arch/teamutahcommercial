Costs Vary by Transaction

These figures are estimates based on typical Utah commercial transactions. Actual closing costs depend on the specific property, lender requirements, title company, negotiated terms, and transaction complexity. Always request a detailed settlement statement from your escrow officer before closing.

Closing Cost Tips

Negotiate Credits

Seller credits toward buyer closing costs are common in commercial deals. Ask your broker about structuring credits into the purchase agreement to reduce your out-of-pocket costs.

Shop Title Companies

Title insurance rates can vary between providers. Request quotes from at least two or three title companies. Some offer commercial discounts on larger transactions.

Understand Your HUD-1

The settlement statement itemizes every cost. Review it carefully at least 3 days before closing. Ask your broker or attorney to explain any line items you do not recognize.

Plan for Reserves

Beyond closing costs, lenders typically require 3-6 months of reserves for debt service, insurance, and taxes. Budget for post-closing cash needs when planning your acquisition.
