Submarket Spotlights

Silicon Slopes

Lehi / Draper Corridor
  • Strong tech-driven office demand with multiple HQ relocations
  • Vacancy under 3% for Class A office space
  • New mixed-use developments adding retail and flex inventory
  • I-15 and FrontRunner access driving premium pricing
Silicon Slopes continues to attract national tech firms relocating operations to Utah. Average office rents have climbed 12% year-over-year, with new spec construction underway along the I-15 corridor between Lehi and Draper. Flex/industrial demand is also surging as e-commerce logistics expand in the area.

Eagle Mountain

Land Development Boom
  • Rapid residential growth creating commercial demand
  • Large development parcels still available at competitive pricing
  • New retail centers planned along SR-73 corridor
  • Infrastructure improvements increasing accessibility
Eagle Mountain's population has more than doubled in the past decade, driving urgent need for neighborhood retail, medical offices, and services. Land prices remain 40-60% below comparable parcels in Salt Lake County, making it a prime target for ground-up development. The upcoming SR-73 widening will further accelerate commercial growth.

Ogden / North Davis

Industrial Growth Corridor
  • Industrial absorption accelerating with logistics demand
  • Price per SF 25-40% below Salt Lake County comparables
  • I-15 / I-84 interchange access for distribution
  • Growing workforce and business-friendly incentives
The Ogden-North Davis industrial corridor has seen record absorption as companies seek lower-cost alternatives to Salt Lake County. With proximity to the I-15/I-84 interchange and competitive labor costs, the area is attracting distribution centers, light manufacturing, and cold storage facilities. Weber County economic incentives have further accelerated tenant migration.
