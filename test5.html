Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to sell commercial property in Utah?
Median time on market for commercial property in Utah is around 4-5 months, but well-priced industrial and retail deals can move in 60-90 days. Our team's marketing approach typically reduces time on market vs. industry averages.
What is a CMA and why do I need one?
A Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) is a free written valuation we provide showing recent comparable sales and a recommended asking price. It's the foundation of any sale decision and helps you avoid leaving money on the table or pricing too high.
Do you work with out-of-state buyers?
Yes. A significant portion of our buyer pool is investors from California, Texas, and the East Coast looking at Utah for population growth, business climate, and tax advantages. We handle remote due diligence and closings routinely.
What are typical commission rates?
Commercial commissions vary by deal size and complexity. We discuss rates upfront on every listing — and we believe in transparency. Call or request a CMA to discuss specifics for your property.
Can you help with 1031 exchanges?
Yes. We've structured many 1031 exchanges for clients, including replacement property identification and coordination with qualified intermediaries. Time is critical with 1031 deadlines — call us early in the process.
